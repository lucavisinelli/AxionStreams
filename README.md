# AxionStreams

Code accompanying:

**L. Visinelli & M. Naydenov, "Transient Axion Streams from Disrupted Miniclusters" (2026), 2605.28005**

This repository studies the formation and evolution of axion streams generated by the tidal disruption of axion miniclusters (AMCs) in the Milky Way.

The code combines:

1. A Monte Carlo treatment of repeated stellar encounters with AMCs, based on the framework developed in Kavanagh et al. (2021).
2. Event-by-event reconstruction of the stripped material produced during tidal disruption.
3. Collisionless tracer evolution of the stripped debris in a Galactic gravitational potential.
4. Statistical analyses of stream morphology, density evolution, encounter probabilities, and direct-detection observables.

## Physical model

The disruption stage follows the semi-analytic Monte Carlo framework of:

B. J. Kavanagh, T. D. P. Edwards, L. Visinelli & C. Weniger,
Phys. Rev. D 104, 063038 (2021).
https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.05377

Individual stellar flybys are sampled along AMC orbits in the Milky Way. The energy injected during each encounter is computed within the impulse approximation and converted into mass loss using precomputed AMC response functions.

The code does not perform direct N-body simulations of tidal disruption. Instead, AMC structural evolution is described using semi-analytic stripping prescriptions calibrated from dedicated calculations.

## Stream reconstruction

For every stripping event, the code stores:

* stripping time,
* stripped mass,
* AMC position and velocity,
* encounter-induced velocity kick,
* longitudinal velocity dispersion,
* transverse velocity dispersion.

The stripped material is converted into tracer packets that sample an anisotropic phase-space distribution around the stripping location.

Tracer particles are subsequently evolved in a fixed Galactic potential using a leapfrog integrator.

Self-gravity of the stripped debris is neglected during the stream-evolution stage.

## Main components

### MonteCarlo.py

Performs Monte Carlo realizations of AMC evolution under repeated stellar encounters.

Outputs:

* surviving AMC properties,
* stripping histories,
* event-by-event debris information.

### stream.py

Constructs tracer realizations of stripped material and evolves them in the Galactic potential.

Outputs:

* stream morphology,
* density evolution,
* covariance matrices,
* density estimators.

### AMC.py

Implements AMC structural evolution and tidal mass-loss prescriptions.

### perturbations.py

Samples stellar encounters and computes impulsive energy injection.

## Example run

```bash
python3 MonteCarlo.py \
    -a 8.5 \
    -N 100000 \
    -profile PL \
    -galaxyID MW \
    -m_a 5e-5 \
    -ID powerlaw
```

This generates AMC disruption histories at a galactocentric radius of 8.5 kpc.

The resulting stripping-event catalog can then be processed by the stream reconstruction routines.

## Reproducing the paper

The repository reproduces the results presented in:

* longitudinal velocity dispersions,
* stream masses,
* stream morphology,
* density evolution,
* stream abundances,
* Solar-neighbourhood encounter probabilities,
* detector-frame linewidths.

Figures 1–10 of the paper are generated from the outputs of the Monte Carlo and stream-reconstruction stages.
